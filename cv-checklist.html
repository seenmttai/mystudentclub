The Chartered Accountant's CV Blueprint

A checklist for precision, impact, and professional polish.

Layout & First Impression

  • One to Two Pages: Maximum two pages for experienced CAs. One is ideal for newly qualified.
  • Clean & Modern Font: Use a single, professional font like Inter, Calibri, or Helvetica (10-12pt).
  • Ample White Space: Avoid dense blocks of text. Use margins and line spacing for readability.
  • Consistent Formatting: Uniform dates, titles, and bullet points throughout the document.
  • PDF Format: Always submit as a PDF named professionally, e.g., FirstName-LastName-CV.pdf.

Header: Your Professional Banner

  • Full Name & Post-Nominals: John Smith, ACA is prominent at the top.
  • Contact Information: A single line with Phone | Professional Email | LinkedIn URL.
  • Location: City or region only (e.g., London, UK). No full home address needed.

Professional Summary: The Executive Overview

  • Concise & Powerful: 3-4 lines maximum, written in the third person or implied first person.
  • Key Credentials: Immediately state you are a Chartered Accountant with X years of experience.
  • Core Specialisms: Mention key areas like Financial Reporting, M&A, Audit, or industry focus.
  • Career Ambition: Briefly state the type of role you are seeking.

Core Competencies: A Scannable Snapshot

  • Bulleted List or Columns: Use 2-3 columns for a clean, scannable list of skills.
  • Technical Skills: Financial Reporting (IFRS/UK GAAP), Financial Modelling, Budgeting & Forecasting, Tax Compliance.
  • Systems Proficiency: SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Advanced Excel, Power BI, Tableau.
  • Commercial Skills: Stakeholder Management, Commercial Acumen, Team Leadership, Process Improvement.

Professional Experience: The Proof of Performance

  • Reverse Chronological Order: Most recent role first.
  • Clear Structure: Job Title | Company Name | Dates (MM/YYYY - MM/YYYY).
  • Achievement-Oriented Bullet Points: Use 3-5 powerful points per role starting with action verbs.
  • Quantify Everything: This is critical for demonstrating impact.

Instead of: "Managed month-end closing."

Use: "Reduced month-end closing cycle from 7 to 4 days by automating reconciliation processes."

Instead of: "Prepared budgets."

Use: "Managed and delivered a £15M annual budget, identifying £750k in cost-saving opportunities."

Qualifications & Education: The Foundation

  • Chartered Accountancy: ACA/ACCA/CIMA Qualification | Institute (e.g., ICAEW) | Year Qualified.
  • Mention first-time passes if applicable.
  • University Degree: Degree Title (e.g., BSc Economics, 2:1) | University Name | Year Graduated.

Final Review: The Zero-Error Check

  • No Typos or Grammatical Errors: Proofread meticulously. Use a tool like Grammarly.
  • Read Aloud: This helps catch awkward phrasing and errors.
  • Check Tense: Past roles in past tense, current role in present tense.
  • Tailored for the Role: Keywords from the job description are reflected in your CV.
  • Peer Review: Ask a trusted colleague or mentor for a final sense check.
